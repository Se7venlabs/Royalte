About the audit
What does Royaltē actually check?▼
Royaltē runs 6 detection modules on your track or artist profile using publicly available metadata from Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube, and MusicBrainz. It checks for metadata consistency, platform coverage gaps, publishing risk indicators, possible duplicate recordings, YouTube and UGC presence, and sync readiness. Each module returns a score and any flags detected.
How accurate is the audit?▼
The audit is based on publicly available metadata — not private royalty statements, not internal distributor data, not rights-chain records. This means our flags indicate likely risks and gaps worth investigating, not confirmed missing money. We score what we can detect. Your distributor or PRO will always be the one to confirm whether a flag is real. Most flags are genuinely worth following up on — but treat them as leads, not guarantees.
What is an audit score?▼
Your audit score is a number out of 100 that reflects how complete and consistent your music's metadata infrastructure is across public sources. A score of 100 means all 6 modules came back clean — correct identifiers, full platform coverage, no publishing risk indicators, no duplicates, good YouTube presence, and strong sync readiness. The average first-time score is 41. Scores below 60 typically mean there are specific things worth fixing.
How long does an audit take?▼
The free preview typically takes 30–60 seconds. The full 6-module report takes 60–90 seconds. This includes resolving your URL, pulling metadata from multiple platforms, running all detection rules, and generating your plain-English recommendations.
Can I audit an album or EP?▼
Currently Royaltē audits individual tracks and artist profiles. Paste a Spotify or Apple Music track URL for a single-track audit, or paste your artist profile URL for an artist-level audit that looks at your overall metadata footprint. Full album and EP batch auditing is coming in a future update.

Flags & results
What does a High severity flag mean?▼
A High severity flag means the issue detected is likely to have a meaningful impact on your music's discoverability, metadata integrity, or publishing setup. Examples include a missing ISRC, no songwriter data found, or a significant platform coverage gap. These are worth actioning as soon as possible with your distributor or PRO.
What is an ISRC and why does it matter?▼
An ISRC (International Standard Recording Code) is a unique identifier for a specific recording of a song. Think of it as a fingerprint for your track. Without an ISRC, streaming platforms and performance rights organisations may not be able to correctly attribute plays to your recording — which can affect royalty reporting. If Royaltē flags a missing ISRC, contact your distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, etc.) and request one be assigned.
What is a publishing risk flag?▼
A publishing risk flag means our engine detected one or more indicators that your publishing setup may have gaps — typically missing songwriter/composer credits in public metadata, identifier conflicts across platforms, or weak metadata infrastructure. This is not a confirmation of missing money. It's a signal that your publishing setup is worth reviewing with your PRO or a music lawyer.
What is sync readiness?▼
Sync readiness scores how discoverable your track is for sync licensing opportunities — placements in TV, film, ads, and games. It looks at whether your metadata is complete, your title is formatted cleanly, you have cross-platform presence, artwork is available, and mood/genre tags exist. A low sync readiness score doesn't mean you can't get sync deals — it means your metadata infrastructure may be making it harder for music supervisors to find and clear your music.
What does the territory coverage audit show?▼
The territory coverage audit cross-references where your music is being streamed against where you appear to have PRO registration. If we detect significant streams in a country but no matching registration signal, we flag it as a possible gap — for example, 980K streams in Germany with no GEMA registration detected.

Important: this is based on publicly available metadata — not your private PRO account. We flag what looks like a gap. Your PRO or distributor confirms whether it's real. Common territories to check include Germany (GEMA), France (SACEM), Brazil (ECAD), Japan (JASRAC), and Scandinavia (STIM/TONO/KODA).
What does "Not Registered" mean in the territory audit?▼
Not Registered means our engine detected streams in that territory but found no matching PRO registration signal in publicly available metadata. This is a flag worth investigating — not a confirmed problem. The first step is to check with your PRO whether you're registered to collect in that territory, and if not, how to register or appoint a sub-publisher.
What does "Pending" mean?▼
Pending means a registration appears to have been initiated but is not yet confirmed in public metadata. PRO registrations can take weeks to process. If a territory shows Pending for more than 30 days, it's worth following up directly with the relevant PRO or your publisher.

Privacy & data
Does Royaltē access my Spotify or Apple Music account?▼
No. Royaltē only uses the public URL you paste. We do not connect to your Spotify for Artists account, your Apple Music for Artists account, or any private data. We pull publicly available metadata only — the same information anyone can see by visiting your artist page.
Does Royaltē see my earnings or royalty statements?▼
No. We have no access to your earnings, royalty statements, distributor payout data, or PRO account information. Everything Royaltē analyses is based on publicly available metadata. This is also why we frame our findings as "likely risks" and "possible gaps" rather than confirmed missing money.
