Artist Identity Confusion

Royaltē refers to this problem as Artist Identity Confusion™.

Artist Identity Confusion™ occurs when digital music platforms, databases, or music services struggle to distinguish between artists who share identical or similar names.

This confusion can affect:

  • Artist profiles
  • Search results
  • Streaming pages
  • Discographies
  • Credits
  • Recommendations
  • Catalog organization

The more platforms your music appears on, the more important accurate identity becomes.

Why Artist Names Alone Aren't Enough

Twenty years ago, artist names were often enough.

Today, millions of songs exist across dozens of streaming services.

Thousands of artists share common names.

Some examples include names like:

  • Phoenix
  • Nova
  • Eclipse
  • Jordan
  • Alex
  • Remedy

Even unique names can eventually be shared by artists in different countries.

Modern music platforms rely on much more than an artist's name to organize catalogs correctly.

What Is Catalog Merging?

One of the most frustrating outcomes of artist identity confusion is Catalog Merging™.

Catalog Merging™ happens when recordings from different artists become grouped together under a single artist profile.

Imagine opening your streaming page and finding:

  • Songs you never recorded.
  • Albums that aren't yours.
  • Another artist's biography.
  • Different profile photos.
  • Incorrect monthly listener statistics.

For fans, it's confusing.

For artists, it can become a serious business problem.

Discovery Becomes More Difficult

Imagine someone searches for your music.

Instead of finding only your catalog, they see another artist with the same name.

Now listeners have to guess.

Which artist is the one they're looking for?

Recommendation engines face the same challenge.

The less confident digital systems are about your identity, the harder it becomes to consistently organize and recommend your music.

That's why artist identity has become a critical part of music discovery.

Can It Affect Royalties?

One of the biggest questions artists ask is:

"Can sharing a name affect my royalties?"

The answer is that identity confusion can create administrative challenges that may require investigation and correction.

For example:

  • Incorrect recording associations
  • Misidentified artist profiles
  • Metadata inconsistencies
  • Catalog attribution issues

Royalties are administered through multiple organizations and systems, each with its own processes and safeguards. While a shared artist name does not automatically mean royalties are paid incorrectly, identity errors can create situations that require attention to ensure recordings and rights information are associated with the correct artist.

The goal is prevention.

The clearer your artist identity, the easier it is for the broader music ecosystem to recognize your catalog correctly.

Metadata Is Your First Line of Defense

Your artist name is only one piece of your identity.

Platforms also rely on information such as:

  • ISRCs
  • Release history
  • Artist identifiers
  • Songwriter information
  • Label information
  • Release dates
  • Contributor information
  • Platform-specific artist IDs

Together, this information creates a much stronger picture of who you are.

Digital Identity™

Royaltē calls this complete picture your Digital Identity™.

Your Digital Identity™ is far more than your stage name.

It's the complete collection of information that tells the global music ecosystem:

  • Who you are
  • Which recordings belong to you
  • Which catalog is yours
  • Which releases are connected
  • Which ownership information is correct

The stronger your Digital Identity™, the easier it becomes for music platforms to distinguish your catalog from someone else's.
