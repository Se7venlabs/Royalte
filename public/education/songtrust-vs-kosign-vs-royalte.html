The Evolution of Music Rights

Twenty years ago, an artist only worried about releasing music.

Today...

Your music exists simultaneously across hundreds of digital platforms, dozens of royalty organizations, multiple countries, countless metadata databases, and an ever-growing ecosystem of streaming services.

Success is no longer just about creating great music.

It's about ensuring your music is correctly identified, registered, distributed, and monitored throughout its entire lifecycle.

Think of your music career like a modern city.

Each platform plays a different role.

Three Different Jobs

There are three completely different problems every artist must solve.

1. Administration

"Who is collecting my publishing royalties?"

2. Intelligence

"Is everything set up correctly so I can actually earn those royalties?"

3. Optimization

"What opportunities am I missing that could increase my revenue?"

No single platform was built to solve every one of those problems.

That's where understanding the differences becomes so valuable.

Songtrust

Category:

Publishing Administration

Best for: Songwriters looking for worldwide publishing administration and royalty collection.

Songtrust acts as your publishing administrator.

Its job is to register your musical compositions with collection societies around the world and collect publishing royalties that may otherwise go unclaimed.

Instead of signing away ownership of your songs, you maintain your copyrights while Songtrust works behind the scenes to administer your publishing rights internationally.

Strengths

✅ Global publishing administration

✅ Performance royalty collection

✅ Mechanical royalty collection

✅ International registrations

✅ Worldwide royalty network

Limitations

Songtrust assumes your information is accurate.

It does not investigate whether:

  • Your metadata is correct.
  • Your artist identities match across platforms.
  • Your catalog is complete.
  • Your releases are missing from storefronts.
  • Your registrations are inconsistent.
  • Revenue opportunities are being lost before collection even begins.

Publishing administration is essential.

But administration only works as well as the information it receives.

KOSIGN

Category:

Modern Publishing Administration

Best for: Independent songwriters looking for publishing administration powered by Kobalt's global infrastructure.

KOSIGN performs many of the same core functions as Songtrust.

It registers songs.

It administers publishing.

It collects royalties worldwide.

Where it differs is its modern onboarding experience and direct connection to Kobalt's extensive publishing network.

Strengths

✅ Publishing administration

✅ Worldwide royalty collection

✅ Global registrations

✅ Kobalt publishing infrastructure

✅ Independent songwriter focus

Limitations

Like Songtrust...

KOSIGN doesn't analyze your music business.

It doesn't perform metadata audits.

It doesn't monitor your catalog.

It doesn't score your music rights health.

It doesn't identify missing registrations before they become lost revenue.

Its mission begins after you've provided your publishing information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Royaltē a publishing administrator?

No. Royaltē is a Music Rights Intelligence™ platform. It helps artists identify metadata issues, identity inconsistencies, catalog gaps, publishing readiness, and potential royalty opportunities. Royaltē does not administer publishing rights or collect royalties on behalf of artists.

Can I use Royaltē with Songtrust?

Yes. Songtrust administers publishing and collects royalties. Royaltē helps ensure your music business is healthy by monitoring metadata, artist identities, registrations, and catalog intelligence. The two services complement one another.

Can I use Royaltē with KOSIGN?

Yes. KOSIGN focuses on publishing administration powered by Kobalt's publishing network. Royaltē provides continuous Music Rights Intelligence™ before, during, and after publishing administration.

Which platform is best for independent artists?

It depends on your needs. If you need publishing administration, Songtrust or KOSIGN may be appropriate. If you want to understand the health of your music business, improve your metadata, monitor your catalog, and identify potential royalty opportunities, Royaltē was designed specifically for that purpose. Many artists benefit from using both.

Doesn't my distributor already do this?

Not entirely. Most distributors focus on delivering music to digital streaming platforms. Publishing administration and Music Rights Intelligence™ address different areas of the music ecosystem that distributors generally do not manage.
