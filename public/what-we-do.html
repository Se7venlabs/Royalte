The Problem

The system behind
your music is invisible.

Most artists know what's happening in front of the microphone. The songs. The releases. The shows. But behind every song is an invisible ecosystem of systems — streaming platforms, publishing databases, metadata registries, licensing authorities, catalog records, and rights networks — that determine whether your music gets found, your royalties get collected, and your professional presence reflects the truth of your work.

Most artists never see that ecosystem. And when something breaks inside it, the damage is silent. A metadata gap on a streaming platform. A missing registration with a licensing authority. A catalog discrepancy that routes royalties to the wrong party.

These aren't technical edge cases. They're the quiet infrastructure failures that cost artists money, discoverability, and control — every day, at scale, invisibly.

How It Works

How Monitoring Works

Royaltē runs a structured intelligence pipeline across every scan — acquiring evidence from trusted industry authorities, detecting changes, and translating findings into clear, actionable intelligence.

01 — ACQUIRE
Evidence Collection

Royaltē queries trusted industry authorities — streaming platforms, publishing databases, metadata registries, catalog sources — to collect a snapshot of your music's current state.

02 — DETECT
Change Detection

Each scan is compared against your last known state. Additions, removals, and modifications are detected — rated by severity and traced to the specific authority where the change occurred.

Coverage

What We Monitor

Royaltē monitors your music across seven authority categories — the same ecosystem represented in every scan, every score, and every Executive Brief™.

Streaming

Availability, listener counts, stream activity, and presence across streaming platforms — the primary commercial evidence of your reach.

Publishing

Mechanical licensing registrations, song codes, and work-level publishing data from statutory licensing authorities.

Metadata

Artist identity, ISNI, MusicBrainz IDs, release data, and canonical metadata accuracy across the industry's authoritative registries.

Catalog

Discography completeness, release data, ISRC coverage, and catalog records across collector and archive databases.

Community

Listener counts, play counts, community tags, and discovery data from community-driven music databases — a separate signal from commercial streaming.

Rights

Ownership verification, ISWC assignment, and rights registration status — foundational evidence for royalty eligibility and catalog integrity.

Digital Presence

Official artist channel status, video presence, and professional digital identity across video and content platforms.
