Imagine Releasing the Greatest Song of Your Career...

You finally finish it.

The writing is incredible.

The production is flawless.

The vocals are perfect.

You release it to the world.

Now imagine...

Nobody knows who owns it.

Every Song Creates Data

The moment your music is released, it begins creating information.

Not just streams.

Data.

Your recording receives an ISRC.

Your release receives a UPC.

Songwriters are identified.

Publishers are connected.

Metadata is distributed.

Streaming services index your music.

Rights organizations begin processing usage information.

Artist profiles connect across platforms.

Every one of these actions creates information that helps the music industry understand what your music is, who owns it, and where royalties should go.

Data Is Your Music's Digital Identity™

Imagine trying to send a letter without a name or address.

It probably wouldn't reach its destination.

Your music works the same way.

Without accurate information, the global music industry has a harder time identifying your recordings, recognizing ownership, and connecting royalties to the correct rights holders.

That's why metadata, registrations, publishing information, and catalog consistency matter.

Together, they form your music's Digital Identity™.

Every one of these organizations depends on accurate information to organize, identify, process, recommend, and report on your music.

Without reliable data, the entire music ecosystem becomes less efficient.

Information Debt Is Real

Most artists never notice data problems.

They're invisible.

A duplicated artist profile.

An incorrect songwriter.

Missing publishing information.

An outdated ISRC.

Metadata inconsistencies.

One issue may seem insignificant.

But over time, those small issues accumulate.

Think of it as Information Debt™.

The longer inconsistencies remain unresolved, the more difficult they can become to identify and organize as your catalog grows.

Your Catalog Never Stops Growing

Your music business evolves constantly.

You release new songs.

Every change creates new information.

Managing that information becomes just as important as managing the music itself.

Because every new release becomes another part of your catalog's Digital Identity™.
